The Gravitational Pipeline

One signal ball B (cyan) threads four gravitational gates in series — a five-body machine that computes a 4-input priority encoder: B's exit port is the index of the first absent control. All four present is a 4-input AND (deepest port); the first gap stops the cascade. Sixteen inputs, five ports, pure Newtonian gravity — compiled by solving the whole coupled trajectory as one boundary-value problem.

What it computes

Each present control bends B one 54° port-step deeper; the first absent one lets B fly straight out its current port. So the exit port = first-zero index — a priority encoder — and 1111 lands the 4-input AND at the deepest port.

The depth wall

Four is the ceiling for this design. A 5th control would fly ~60 units through the whole accumulated field, get chaotically deflected, and can't be aimed onto B — its calibration goes blind. Chaos bounds computational depth, not just precision.

The chaos tax

A 1e-9 nudge to B's launch grows ~1.1 digits per gate — 4.4 digits across the chain (staircase, lower left). Precision alone would allow ~13 gates; aiming caps it first at ~5.
